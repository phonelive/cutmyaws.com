# Hiring Plan: First 1099 Contractor

## Role: Senior AWS Implementation Contractor

Execute optimizations from David's Reports. David writes the plan, they run the plays. No report writing, no client calls, no unsupervised architectural decisions.

## Why

Fix implementations stack — by month 5-6, David will have 4-6 concurrent Fix engagements. That's the capacity bottleneck, not Reports or sales.

## When: Month 4-5 (Jun-Jul 2026)

**Trigger to hire** (any 2 of these):
- 3+ Fix engagements signed or in pipeline
- Turning down or delaying work because of capacity
- Spending more than 30 hrs/week on implementation (vs. sales/reports)
- Revenue has crossed $150K cumulative

**Trigger to wait:**
- Fewer than 2 Fix clients
- Pipeline is thin
- Still figuring out the implementation playbook

## Profile

- 5+ years hands-on AWS (not just certified — actually built things)
- Strong in EC2, Lambda, RDS, networking, IAM
- Has done cost optimization work before (or at least understands the levers)
- Comfortable working async with a checklist and a Slack channel
- US-based preferred (client trust, timezone, ITAR-safe)

## 1099-Safe Structure

| 1099-Safe | W-2 Red Flag |
|-----------|-------------|
| Project-based scope ("implement these 12 items from the Report") | Open-ended "work for me indefinitely" |
| They set their own hours | You dictate schedule |
| They use their own tools/laptop | You provide equipment |
| They can work for other clients | Exclusivity requirement |
| Defined deliverables + completion | Ongoing duties |
| Invoice per project or milestone | Biweekly paycheck |

Structure each Fix engagement as a separate SOW:
> "Implement items 1-12 from [Client] Report. Deliverables: completed optimizations + change log. Timeline: 4-6 weeks. Fee: $X."

## Compensation

**Option A: Hourly ($125-175/hr)**
- Simple, predictable for them
- You eat the risk if the Fix takes longer than expected
- Good for testing the relationship

**Option B: Revenue share (20-30% of your Fix fee)**
- Aligns incentives — they get paid more when savings are bigger
- You keep 70-80% for doing the Report + selling + managing
- On a $53K Fix fee: they get $11-16K per engagement
- Better long-term, but harder to sell to the contractor initially

**Recommendation:** Start with hourly for the first 1-2 engagements. Once trust is established, shift to revenue share.

|--|--|--|

| Max concurrent Fix engagements | 2-3 | 5-7 |
| Fix conversion bottleneck | Capacity-limited | Sales-limited |

## Brand Protection

Your brand is "Just David." When you bring someone on:
- Clients should still feel like they're getting David
- You write the Report, run the calls, sign off on every change
- Position the contractor as "my implementation engineer" — not a separate entity
- QA every Fix before 90-day verification
